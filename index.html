I am a PhD Scholar at the School of Law and Government, Dublin City University where I am a recipient of the Ireland India Institute Fellowship. Under the supervision of Dr Jivanta Schöttli and Prof Maura Conway, I am working on how ordinary women participate in politics. For my thesis, I study women in the Anti-CAA/NRC Protests in India.

Broadly, my areas of interest include political movements, participation, and active citizenship. I hold an MA in Peace and Conflict Studies from TISS, where I graduated as Institute Gold Medallist, and a BA (Honours) in Political Science from St. Xavier's College, Kolkata. Additionally, I work with the North Eastern Social Research Centre, India, on peacebuilding and women’s rights.

Outside academia, I enjoy writing on political issues and popular culture, traveling to unmapped cities, and street photography.
