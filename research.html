Books

Both volumes are the result of sustained fieldwork — including in active conflict zones across Northeast India — and the long labour of building research from the ground up. They are available in print.

Landscape of Conflicts and Peace in the Northeast: The Role of Religion

Fernandes, W., Ghosh, K., et al. · NESRC Peace Studies Series, Vol. 14 · 2019

This volume examines the relationship between religion and conflict in Northeast India from a sociological perspective — asking not whether religion causes conflict, but how and by whom it is mobilised. Drawing on fieldwork across four states of the Northeast, the contributions show that religion functions as a powerful component of social formation, one that the powerful deploy to sustain conflicts, but that others draw upon equally for peacemaking.

Women and Peacebuilding: Perspectives from the Field

Tula, M., & Ghosh, K. (Eds.) · NESRC Peace Studies Series, Vol. 15 · 2021

This volume emphasises the multi-faceted participation of women in the social and political life of communities in Northeast India and elsewhere. The contributions make evident the varied modalities through which women's position in contexts of conflict and peacebuilding may be experienced, studied, and understood — bringing new perspectives to bear on women's political agency and aspirations beyond binary frames of gendered identity.
